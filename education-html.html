Wind Ensemble and Jazz Band

Music has been a passion of mine for over 12 years now. I have had the honor of playing for a number of great school programs and in each have striven for excellence. I play the trombone
and the euphonium; I primarily play jazz on trombone and exclusivley play classical on the euphonium. At loyola, I played as the lead trombone in the jazz band my senior year and was a constant example and leader for the three years I was in the band. For most of my time in the wind ensemble, I was the only euphonium player. This gave me
the opportunity to grow in my capacity to improve without peer feedback and to gauge my success to my own growth and accomplishment. I was constantly leading the low brass section and setting the example for the level of excellence I believe the band could perform at. I had the pleasure to perform many solos and a number of small group performances which allowed me to grow in my musical ability, my ability to prepare for deadlines and public performance, and my ability to maintain my composure in high pressure situations. I am thankful for my time in the band program at Loyola University and left with a legacy of leadership, standard setting, and greatness.
